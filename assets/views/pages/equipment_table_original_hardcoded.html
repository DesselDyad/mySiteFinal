Rope A rope can be extremely useful in a large variety of situations. Whether one needs to climb up or down, over or under something, just needs to tie something together or pull something, ropes are a essential item on your list.
Pocket knife Being on the move all the time, with limited ressources, means one is going to have to improvise quite a bit. Whether it's cutting something, opening a can, being in need of a screwdriver or whatever else, you're going to have quite a lot of use for a pocket knife. Just like rope it's going to be almost indispensable.
Canteen One never knows how frequent one will have access to drinkable water, so having a way to carry water as well as rationing it is vital to surviving.
Sleeping bag I wont bother writing too much here, as i doubt fairly seriously that anyone needs any real explanation as to the benefits of a sleeping bag. I'll only advice to look for one as compact and simultaneously as warm as possible, as you won't have too much space on you to carry it, but at the same time the nights are likely to be cold.
Sleeping pad Sleeping mats need even less explaining, their entire purpose is for one to lie on them and being just a little bit more comfortable.
Backpack I advice everyone to get a larger backpack, with lots of space for all the stuff you're going to be wanting to carry around. It should be just about as big as possible while still being comfortable to carry around all day. I would recommend something inbetween a standard military backpack and a regular backpacking backpack. Also, the more possibilities it has for strapping stuff on the outside, e.g. ones sleeping pad, bag and cooking equipment, the better.
Compass Every kind of electronics is going to be running out of power fairly quickly, so that won't be of much use - be it a cell-phone, a walk-man or whatever. Of cause, one can use the sun and the stars to poinpoint locations and directions, but in my opinion it really wouldn't hurt to have a compass. It takes up virtually no space and has the potential to be incredibly useful.
Fire Starter/Zippo I don't really think i need to stress the importance of being able to create fire, be it for the benefits of light, heat or being able to prepare food - not to mention all kinds of other uses.
Cooking Equipment As well as being fairly useful when preparing meals, it provides the illusion of some civilization being preserved.
FLashlight Given the lack of electricity, it's going to be quite dark quite a large amount of the time. That makes lightsources incredibly valuable - and do remember to collect batteries whenever possible, you never know. Plus, again, they take up virtually 0 space.

Clothes

Picture Item Description
Jacket The choice of clothes might be more important than one might think. They need to be warm, comfortable, as light as possible, as water-resistant and quick-drying as possible, be as tough as possible in case zombies attack you as well as preferably be dark or even having a camouflage pattern. The more of those attributes it possesses, the longer you'll be out and about.
Trousers Basically all the same things that apply to jackets also apply to pants - they should be able to handle both wind, wheather, zombies and everything else that might come up in this context.
Boots The choice of footwear is probably far more important than most people realize. I strongly advice against any kind of sneakers, trainers and the likes - they are going to be falling apart very quickly, as they aren't suited for prolonged outdoor terrain use, and they wont provide you with any help. Boots, in comparison, will provide secure footing, about the best possible foot-climate possible under the circumstances, they are usually pretty water-resistant and also zombies wont as easily bite through them.
Protective gloves Dealing with zombies, ones hands are extremely exposed to being bitten, or just to blood-spatter. Because of this, i advice everyone to aquire protective gloves, both against the cold, bites and blood.
Protective vest For one thing zombies will have a tougher time biting you, but also, should you run into other survivors - for the purpose of this example, hostile ones - your chances to come out on top and victoriously walk away are much bigger than without.
Protective pads Zombies love to bite you in your shoulders, neck and the like, everything that's about the same height as they are. The more armor you got around there, the better.

First-aid-kit

Picture Item Description
First-aid-kit I don't know whether listing an actual medkit at this point is just nothing but redundant, as a medkits content is more or less comprised of the following number of items anyway. In any case, without one, you won't make it for very long out there. You get sick, you get slow, you get un-dead.
Aspirin While weak, simple painkillers aren't really vitally important, they are extremely nice to have. Especially considering the various causes of headaches and the like that one is bound to encounter in a world like this.
penicilin It might not cure you, if you get bitten, but in case you're running a fever or otherwise get sick, you're going to be dependent on either medicine or luck. And personally, i'd rather go with medicine on this one.
Bandages Band-aids, or just bandages in general, might be fairly limited, but are going to eventually be extremely convenient. Also, they can be improvised by ripping up old clothes.
Sciccors I think this one really doesn't require any further explanation.
Morphine If you break or leg, need to amputate something, or find yourself in a similar situation where strong painkillers are needed, then morphine is probably the gold standard.
Desinfectant Whether it can stop a bite depends on the kind and strength of the individual desinfectant, but if it's "just" a normal wound which, say, causes concern for good old basic blood poisoning, jsut about any desinfectant is going to come in extremely handy. Not to mention how extremely bad hygiene is going to be, which makes it extremely valuable all by itself.
